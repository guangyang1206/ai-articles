From "tech utopia" to "capital game" — a 21-day, 5x valuation story

In May 2026, China's primary market was set ablaze by a single piece of news.

DeepSeek — the AI lab long known for its "no funding, no IPO, no commercialization" stance — has officially launched its first-ever external equity round.

The company is seeking to raise up to 50 billion yuan (~$7.3 billion), valuing it at around 350 billion yuan ($51.5 billion) post-money.[1][2]

Even more striking: founder Liang Wenfeng plans to personally contribute up to $2.8 billion, accounting for 40% of the total raise.[1][3]

Just 21 days earlier, market estimates had DeepSeek at roughly $10 billion, with an initial plan to raise only $300 million. In three weeks, valuation jumped 5x and the fundraising target ballooned 24x.[4]

PART 1

🌌 The Idealist's "Three No's"

Born in 1985 in Zhanjiang, Guangdong, Liang Wenfeng holds degrees from Zhejiang University. He started in quantitative finance: in 2008, his 7-person team used machine learning for quant trading, achieving 500% returns in three months.

He founded High-Flyer Quant in 2015, which grew to manage over 100 billion yuan by 2021. In 2025, High-Flyer delivered a 56.6% average return, netting Liang over $700 million that year alone.[4] He made the Forbes China Rich List with an $11.5 billion fortune, ranking 34th.[5]

But Liang's ambition went beyond finance.

In March 2020, High-Flyer made a highly unconventional decision — pouring profits into building its own GPU cluster."Firefly 1" carried thousands of high-end GPUs, laying the groundwork for what would become DeepSeek.[4]

In July 2023, DeepSeek was officially founded. Liang established three iron rules: no outside funding, no IPO, and no commercialization.

"DeepSeek's goal is to build world-class general-purpose AI models — not to make money, not to go public. Capital chases short-term returns; commercialization compromises technical direction."[4]

In the 2023-2024 AI gold rush, this was a near-rebellious stance. The Paper (澎湃新闻) called DeepSeek "not the celestial army, but a maverick Zhenyuanzi" — technically strong, stubbornly independent, refusing to pick sides.

Then came the technical payoff:

May 2024 — DeepSeek-V2 launched with an innovative MoE architecture.

Dec 2024 — DeepSeek-V3 open-sourced with a 53-page technical report.

Jan 2025 — DeepSeek-R1 matched OpenAI o1 on math, code, and reasoning. Published in Nature. Training cost: just $294,000.[6]

The "Three No's" was celebrated as proof that world-class AI could thrive without venture capital.

PART 3

⚡ Why Take the Money? Three Pressures

🔹 Compute: From Algorithmic Brilliance to a War of Attrition

Two years ago, LLM contests could be won with clever algorithms. Today, it's a raw compute war. As 36Kr put it bluntly: "High-Flyer has deep pockets, but not deep enough for a compute arms race against global giants."[4]

🔹 Talent: Star Researchers Are Leaving

Since H2 2025, at least five core researchers have left:

• Guo Daya → ByteDance as Agent lead

• Luo Fuli → Xiaomi, poached by Lei Jun

• Wang Bingxuan, Wei Haoran also departed

• Meta's offer: 4-year contracts worth $200-300M total

The funding round is partly about creating option pools to retain talent.

🔹 Productization: The Lab Must Go to Market

DeepSeek employees have begun pitching models to enterprise clients. V4.1's MCP protocol support is explicitly designed for enterprise integration.

A lab can focus on benchmarks. A serious AI company must focus on customers, revenue, delivery, and cost structure.

PART 4

♟️ A Carefully Orchestrated Capital Play

$2.8 Billion

Liang personal investment — 40% of total raise, making him the largest investor

$2.8 Billion for What? Control.

A founder personally investing $2.8 billion is virtually unprecedented in global AI. Industry insiders told KCB that the capital likely involves bank credit lines.[13]

Had he participated only as a minority co-investor, external investors would have gained far more pricing power over the company's direction.The $2.8 billion buys the right to dominate conversation at this valuation.

Only Two Kinds of Money Accepted

China IC Fund (Big Fund) in talks to lead — strategic, won't interfere with technical direction

Tencent investing ~$850M for ~2% equity — industrial capital for ecosystem synergy

Traditional VCs (Sequoia, Hillhouse, etc.) reportedly all turned away. Alibaba failed to reach terms.[4]

Lock Down Ownership First, Then Open the Door

On April 27, Liang boosted his direct stake from 1% to 34%, giving him total effective control of approximately 84.29%.No matter how much external capital enters, Liang's control is locked in.[7][8]

PART 5

🏛️ The Big Picture: China's "Big Five" LLM Landscape

Between May 7-14, Chinese LLM companies rewrote valuation records in a single week:

May 7 — Moonshot AI (Kimi) closed ~$2B Series D, topping $20B valuation. Lead: Meituan Longzhu.

May 8 — Stepfun reported ~$2.5B funding with supply-chain investors, fast-tracking HK IPO.

May 13 — Zhipu AI jumped 36.9% in HK trading, market cap exceeding HK$500B; MiniMax rose 18.46%.[13]

Zhipu AI — 2025 revenue under RMB 800M, market cap >HK$500B

iFlytek — RMB 27.1B revenue, RMB 5.2B net profit, market cap ≈ RMB 118.7B

A loss-making Zhipu was valued at nearly 4x profitable iFlytek.

The shift in capital sources from "US dollar + internet" to "state capital + supply chain" tells a deeper story: LLMs are no longer just a commercial bet — they are a critical lever for national tech competitiveness.[13]
